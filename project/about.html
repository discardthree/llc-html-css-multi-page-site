Stewed crabapples

  • 14 pints crabapples
  • 14 cups water
  • 8.5 cups sugar
  • 16-20 cloves

(or if you don't have ridiculous quantities, the ratios are 2 cups crabapples : 1 cup water : a little less than 2/3 cup sugar : 1 or 2 cloves)

  1. Put everything but the crabapples in a pot and stir over low heat to dissolve the sugar.
  2. Prep the crabapples by washing them and stabbing through each one with a toothpick or bamboo skewer.
    (This helps them cook evenly so they are more likely to stay intact.)
  3. Add the crabapples to the pot, and add a little more water if needed to make the water level at least close to covering.
  4. Cook covered over a very low simmer until the point where the crabapples are softened and the peels have torn, but the fruit has not dissolved to mush.
  5. They can be canned at this point, but will keep quite well in the fridge.
--Grandma Flo
